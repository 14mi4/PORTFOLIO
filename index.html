I’m a multi-skilled creative professional with a unique blend of skills that range from graphic design to web development. I graduated from the School of Applied Arts in La Chaux-de-Fonds (CH) in 2023, then decided to follow a web development course in order to acquire coding skills. My expertise is vast and varied, allowing me to work across a wide spectrum of creative and technical fields.

Whether it’s designing for print, crafting web interfaces, enhancing user experiences, or developing web applications, I bring a versatile approach to every project. I’m more passionate about front-end in general, UI/UX design, illustration, and publishing. I thrive on the challenge of blending form and function to create engaging, visually compelling solutions.

Beyond my professional life, I find inspiration in music—particularly the nu-metal genre and the iconic trends of the early 2000s and 2010s. These elements, along with my love for pop culture, deeply influence my aesthetic and creative style. When I’m not working on projects, you’ll likely find me either diving into the latest trends, hiking or walking by the lake, drawing, gaming, or indulging in the nostalgia of the 2000s.

My goal is to continue evolving as a creative, blending my diverse skill set with my passions to produce work that resonates both visually and emotionally.

02

DIGITAL ATLAS

Digital Atlas is a fictional website offering an immersive exploration of Ukraine's rich cultural heritage, highlighting iconic monuments dispersed across the country. Users can navigate the site in various ways: through a classic menu, an interactive map, or by browsing images. The left sidebar remains fixed for easy access to different sections. This project aims to provide an engaging and educational experience, allowing users to discover Ukraine's historical landmarks and cultural treasures in a user-friendly and visually appealing format.

Field: Web design
Medium: Website
Format: Desktop / Mobile
Year: 2023

05

HAMMURABI CODEX

Centered on the exploration of the concept of monumentality through an in-depth study of the laws of the ancient city of Babylon, this tiny book is heavily inspired by the significance of monuments in ancient society, the focus is on the laws that governed the daily lives of the inhabitants of this legendary city. Printed in white on black to symbolize the basalt stone in which these laws were originally inscribed. This project not only delves into the monumental importance of these laws but also pays tribute to their enduring impact on human civilization.

Field: Editorial design
Medium: Book
Dimensions: Pocket (109 x 154 mm)
Printing: Digital
Binding: Glued / Japanese (handmade)
Year: 2023

09

GORE VS BUSH

A newspaper that provides a comprehensive exploration of the 2000 U.S. presidential election, featuring a detailed comparison between Al Gore and George W. Bush. The layout immerses readers in the electoral debate, compiling all relevant information about the candidates, their policies, and key campaign issues. It includes a full transcript of the debates and detailed electoral statistics, offering an accurate perspective on the trends and outcomes of this historic election. The newspaper serves as an in-depth resource for understanding the complexities and significance of the 2000 presidential race.

Medium: Newspaper
Dimensions: Broadsheet (597 x 749 mm)
Printing: Offset
Binding: /
Year: 2021
